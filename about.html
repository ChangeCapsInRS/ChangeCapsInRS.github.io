From Scene Change to Report Generation: Generating Change Summaries from Remote Sensing Image Pairs

About

Image Processing, in our case particularly using pairs of Sentinel 1 and Sentinel 2 images, offers a profound insight into dynamic changes occurring across landscapes over time. These images serve as invaluable tools for various applications, from environmental monitoring to urban planning and disaster management. By leveraging the unique capabilities of each satellite, such as Sentinel 1's radar imagery for monitoring changes in land cover and Sentinel 2's multispectral imagery for detailed land classification, our AI can detect alterations in vegetation, water bodies, urban infrastructure, and more. The time difference between image pairs allows for the identification and analysis of temporal patterns, facilitating the understanding of long-term trends and short-term events, essential for decision-making processes.

One significant application of Image Processing is Image Captioning, where automated systems generate descriptive captions for images. This technology holds promise in aiding analysts by quickly summarizing the content of vast image datasets. Evaluating the quality of generated captions is crucial for assessing the performance of such systems. Metrics like BLEU (Bilingual Evaluation Understudy), METEOR (Metric for Evaluation of Translation with Explicit Ordering), CIDEr (Consensus-based Image Description Evaluation), and ROUGE (Recall-Oriented Understudy for Gisting Evaluation) provide quantitative measures of captioning quality by comparing generated captions to human-written references. By employing a database of manually captioned images and evaluating the outputs of their captioning tool against these metrics, our researchers iteratively improve the system's accuracy and relevance.

Through the synergy of satellite image processing and image captioning, experts can gain actionable insights into changes occurring within landscapes, empowering informed decision-making across various domains. By harnessing the power of these technologies and continually refining their methodologies through rigorous evaluation, analysts can unlock the full potential of satellite imagery, contributing to a more sustainable and resilient future.

Why Use AI for Image Processing?

Using image processing AI tools to identify changes between paired images with time differences offers unparalleled efficiency and accuracy compared to manual methods. These advanced algorithms swiftly analyze vast amounts of visual data, detecting even subtle alterations with precision. Unlike manual inspection, which is time-consuming and prone to human error, AI tools ensure consistent results and significantly reduce workload. Moreover, they can handle complex image transformations and variations in lighting or perspective, enhancing their reliability. Leveraging AI for image analysis not only streamlines the process but also unlocks deeper insights into temporal changes, making it indispensable across various fields like Flood Detection, Urban Change, and Environmental Monitoring.

Our Areas of Study:

Image Captioning: From narrating the visual narrative to augmenting accessibility, we employ the prowess of AI to articulate descriptions for images, fostering inclusivity and enhancing comprehension across diverse audiences.

Satellite Image Processing: Leveraging the rich data streams from Sentinel 1 and Sentinel 2 satellites, we delve into the intricate realm of image processing to unveil insights crucial for various applications. With Sentinel 1's radar imagery providing all-weather, day-and-night monitoring capabilities, and Sentinel 2's multispectral imagery offering high-resolution views, our endeavors encompass a wide spectrum of tasks. From detecting environmental changes such as vegetetetion growth and water level increase to assessing urban sprawl, our approach merges the prowess of AI with satellite imagery to navigate the complexities of our dynamic planet. Through advanced algorithms and machine learning techniques, we transform raw satellite data into actionable information, empowering decision-makers in sustainable resource management, disaster mitigation, and urban planning endeavors.
